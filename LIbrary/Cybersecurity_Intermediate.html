HERE IS A ROAD MAP FOR AN INTERMEDIATE TO LEARN CYBERSECURITY

Intermediate Learners (8 Weeks)

Week 1: Deep Dive into Cybersecurity Assets

Objective: Gain a deeper understanding of cybersecurity assets and their protection mechanisms.

  • Read advanced materials on asset management and classification.
  • Conduct a personal audit of digital assets and develop a protection plan.

Week 2-3: Advanced Threats and Attack Techniques

Objective: Explore advanced persistent threats (APTs) and emerging attack vectors.

  • Study advanced threat intelligence reports and analysis.
  • Participate in online forums and discussions on emerging cybersecurity threats.

Week 4-5: Advanced Detection and Response

Objective: Learn about advanced detection systems and incident response methodologies.

  • Install and configure open-source security tools for network and host-based detection.
  • Practice incident response scenarios in a simulated environment (e.g., using platforms like CyberRange).

Week 6: Cybersecurity Frameworks and Standards

Objective: Understand major cybersecurity frameworks and their applications.

  • Study materials on popular cybersecurity frameworks (e.g., NIST Cybersecurity Framework, ISO/IEC 27001).
  • Analyze case studies of organizations implementing cybersecurity frameworks.

Week 7-8: Project and Practical Application

Objective: Apply knowledge and skills to a practical cybersecurity project.

  • Develop a comprehensive cybersecurity plan for a personal project or hypothetical scenario.
  • Document the process, challenges faced, and lessons learned during the project.
